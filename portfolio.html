My Journey Through Pain, Perseverance, and Purpose

Life threw its hardest punch at me on 17th July, leaving me with a completely damaged knee ligament and bone. An accident that not only grounded me physically but also threatened to shatter my dreams. I missed my MSE, scoring a devastating 0 marks, and was sentenced to three months of bed rest, grappling with excruciating pain that tested the limits of my endurance.But amidst the darkness, I found my purpose. Lying on that bed, unable to move, I guided 3,000 MHT CET students in their counseling journey. Some of them made it to WCE, and their victories became my motivation. As my knee began its slow recovery, on 10th October, I bent it for the first time in three months and stood again. It wasn’t just a physical stance—it was my stand against defeat.

Returning to college at the end of October, I faced a mountain of challenges. With no idea about the subjects and academic lectures nearing their end, I knew I had to push myself harder than ever. I worked 7 hours a day in the lab, despite the pain and limitations, giving it my all.Some of my friend also help me a lot and I cleared all my labs, NPTEL courses, and subjects with good marks.But life had one more twist for me. In one subject, I fell short by just 3 marks, and grace marks didn’t come my way. Yet, I hold my head high. Why? Because I gave it everything I had. I didn’t let the worst phase of my life define me. I still found time and strength to help juniors through their tough times, even as I battled my own challenges.

This journey has taught me that success isn’t just about marks or results—it’s about showing up, persevering, and giving your absolute best, no matter the odds. I have no regrets because I know I’ve done all I could, and the result is the best I could achieve under the circumstances.And still I don't think that my pain was too much in front of some of the peoples which I saw in hospital where I was admitted that time!Remember this: The strongest steel is forged in the hottest fire. And I’ve emerged stronger, unbroken, and ready for whatever comes next. Thank you for reading my story—may it inspire you to face your challenges head-on and never back down!

My Journey through JEE and MHT-CET

JEE is not just an exam; it holds the dreams of millions of students. And behind every dream lies a unique story. Here’s mine—a story of hope, struggles, and persistence.

I was a bright student, securing 97% in 9th and 98% in 10th, and my JEE journey began at the end of 11th grade. Coming from a small town, Lasalgaon in Nashik, I had little exposure to the competitive world outside. In my town, hardly anyone knew about JEE, except for the fact that it was the gateway to NITs and IITs. My father, a teacher, was aware of the exam's significance and dreamed of me becoming an IITian.When COVID struck, everything changed. To support my studies, my parents decided to shift to Nashik, 65-70 km away, leaving behind our village. My father began commuting daily between Lasalgaon and Nashik—a grueling 4-hour journey—to ensure my sister and I had a better chance at education. My parents made incredible sacrifices, and seeing their dedication, I promised myself to make their efforts worthwhile.

Initially, I joined a reputed coaching institute online. But when offline classes started, things began to fall apart. The chemistry teacher left, and for two months, there were no lectures. Physics was still online, and only mathematics had proper classes. By this time, I had also suffered from Chikungunya, which set me back significantly.Trying to recover from the backlog while attending 12-hour-long coaching sessions was overwhelming. The institute’s strict homework system added further pressure—they made us rewrite incomplete homework ten times, and even called parents to complain about it. My parents, already stressed from the sacrifices they were making, became disappointed. They started doubting their decision to move and began questioning my abilities. My father’s sad expressions, coupled with my mother’s concern, broke me. I felt like I was failing them, and I couldn’t even share how bad things were at the institute because it was for me that they had uprooted their lives.

The situation at the institute only worsened. Chemistry teachers kept leaving—seven in total—and the constant disruption left us with no continuity. Event remaining teachers divided between two centers. By December 2022, just a month before the first JEE Main attempt, I was left feeling unprepared and helpless. My body trembled with fear during the exam. When the results came out, they were far below my expectations. That was the breaking point. I decided to leave the coaching institute and create my own study plan. With the help of YouTube, HC Verma, and other books, I started self-studying. I finally found peace in my preparation, and every night I went to bed feeling satisfied. My confidence returned, and I regained hope. Then came the HSC Board exams. I studied for each paper the night before, pulling 18-hour sessions, and managed to score 81.17%. This result boosted my motivation. I made an ambitious plan to achieve 99% in the second JEE Main attempt and worked tirelessly for it. However, life had other plans.

Seeing my struggles and wanting the best for me, my parents decided to enroll me in Nashik's top institute for an MHT-CET crash course. I tried to convince them to give me another chance at JEE, promising I would crack it. But their decision was final. And so began my MHT-CET journey—a new chapter, a new challenge, and a renewed determination to succeed.Still regretting sometimes that I do not got second chance to prove myself in JEE but I never lost by my mind and hardwork which I put during my whole journey and to prove myself I had put too much efforts and made 98% in MHT CET and getting into WCE Sangli.

This journey, from my lowest point to regaining hope, taught me resilience, hard work, and the importance of self-belief. Exam preparation not only teaches you about how to study but also pressure handling , time management, hardworking, strong willpower, positive thinking and more, also not the destination that defines success but the courage to rise every time you fall.Many students got demotivated in this period but I always like to give an example of patients Heartbit graph which continuously going up and down , if it remains same or straight then patient's existance will be over and its like real life.In this preparation big things is to stay strong in competition and give your best. My story is far from over, and I’m determined to turn every struggle into a stepping stone toward success.

A Milestone Worth Remembering...

Today, I received the very first earning of my life—a moment that every student dreams of. And without a second thought, I dedicated it to my parents. Even though I am miles away and couldn’t hand it to them personally, just knowing that it reached them and witnessing their happiness has been the most fulfilling experience of my journey so far.

What makes this milestone even more meaningful is how I achieved it—not merely by completing a task, but by contributing to someone’s life in a way that truly mattered. I had the privilege of helping someone make life-changing decisions, and the trust they placed in me is something I value far beyond the financial reward. The skills I developed, the growth I experienced, and the responsibility I embraced are treasures no money can measure.

This achievement also reminds me of the times when I was preparing for exams and didn’t even have money to buy a book. In those difficult moments, there were people who stood beside me, who supported me, and who believed in me. Today, being able to return to them—giving back with my own hard-earned income—makes me feel proud and deeply grateful. Their small investment in me has now become one of the best decisions, and I am honored to have proved their belief right.

Some of those helping hands may no longer be in this world, but their kindness and blessings live forever in my heart. I carry their memories with me, and every success of mine is also a tribute to them. But above all, this milestone belongs to my parents. Whatever I am today, and whatever I will ever become, is because of their endless sacrifices, unconditional love, and constant belief in me. Every step forward is a reflection of their strength and guidance.

It doesn’t matter how big or small the first earning is—what matters is the joy of giving it back to the people who have given us everything. To me, this moment is not just about money—it’s about gratitude, love, and the values that keep us grounded.To my parents, my supporters, and those who once helped me when I had nothing—you are my true inspiration. This is just the beginning, and everything I achieve will always carry your blessings.
